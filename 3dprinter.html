3D Printer

3D PRINTERS - a gift to Mechanical and Civil Engineering Students

Reality is always different from virtual. Students from Mechanical and Civil branches build their 3d-models in soft-wares like AUTOCAD and SOLIDWORKS. If they had a chance to see their model in the real world, they can develop their thinking way to match the real case scenarios.

What is 3D Printing?

3D Printing creates a 3 dimensional plastic solid based on a computer generated Stereo Lithography (.STL) File. You can create a .stl file in almost any modeling program that can generate 3D objects (Rhino, Revit, AutoCad, FormZ, Viz, 3Ds-Max, etc.).

Here are some parameters you should keep in mind when creating your .stl file.

Size - The maximum size you can print is: 8" x 10" x 8"

Scaling - We require that all .stl files are in inches and scaled to the desired print size. This scaling can be performed in any 3D modeling program and should be done prior to bringing the file down to the lab. Keep in mind the scale you would like to print your model when you begin 3D modeling; a 1/16" of a 3D printed model at 1'=1/32" scale is 6" thick full scale.

Thickness and Shape - The 3D printer uses a cementations material that handles compression very well, but does not do well in tension. You should therefore design your model appropriately. The performance of the material can be improved using glue-based post-processing, but this still requires that you are able to remove the model from its immersed condition without breaking it. In general, we recommend a minimum feature size of about of 1/16".

Drainage - If your model has hollow cavities, we require providing a drain hole so that you can drain out the cellulose powder during post processing. Otherwise you will need to pay for the powder filling those internal voids.

Objects - For optimal placement and pricing you should have one solid object per .stl file.

Model Price

Model cost is the sum of the price of the powder material and the binder material. A solid model that is 5x7" and 1/16" thick will cost approximately Rs 2400. The price of your model can be significantly decreased by hollowing out solid pieces.

Build Time

The key dimension that determines build time is the model height. For every inch of vertical height, the build time will be approximately 1 hour, plus two hours of drying time at the end. When your .stl file is submitted to IO staff you will receive an estimate for when the print will be done that includes the following factors: the height of your model, other students in queue and always adds an additional two hours for the drying. We cannot guarantee completion of models submitted less than 48 hours before a desired deadline.

Pros: Easy and free; you are done immediately. If your model is sufficiently strong and thick this is a good option for you. The model will remain bright white in color.

Cons: The 3D print model material is bonded with a binder that is water-soluble; this means that if it gets wet the integrity of the glue will be compromised.
