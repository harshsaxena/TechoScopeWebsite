Internet Connectivity

So what exactly is an optical fiber?

An optical fiber is a flexible, transparent fiber made of high quality extruded glass (silica) or plastic, which is slightly thicker than a strand of human hair. It can function as a waveguide (light pipe), to transmit light between the two ends of the fiber. These multi utility fiber wires use fiber-optic communications, where they permit transmission over longer distances and at higher bandwidths (data rates) than wire cables. With less loss and immunity to electromagnetic interference, these wires are the future of information transmission.

Why do we need it in VIT campus?

Well the simple answer is that we need it for faster WIFI connections. In the current scenario, the WIFI speed is very slow and at times is cumbersome to work with. In a university like VIT where everything revolves around the internet, from student login to the soft wares that we use in our labs, faster and better internet connectivity is the need of the hour. Thus to increase the work efficiency of students better WIFI is a must. By using optical fiber we could Increase our WIFI speed up to 25Mbps.

It's not just the WIFI, our lab networks aren't up to the mark as well. System problems such as crash and system drape are known glitches to students. This technology can provide the required stability to the lab networks.

The only problem is the connectivity of the fibers themselves. Joining these fiber is more complex than joining electrical wires or cables. The ends of the fibers must be carefully cleaved and then carefully spliced together so the cores align perfectly. If we ever do plan to opt for optical fiber technology in VIT, what is it going to cost? Even though this piece of technology is great, yet it comes with its cost. A meter of optical fiber costs around Rs.7ooo. Considering the expanse of the VIT campus, the expenditure might reach up to 2.0 to 3cr rupees. For a onetime investment this should not be a concern for a university like VIT.
